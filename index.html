Rule: if you learned the information from a source, cite it. This is true even when you put the information into your own words.

Being an EMT or paramedic means dealing with emergencies and helping people right away. The Bureau of Labor Statistics says EMTs and paramedics “provide emergency medical care” and sometimes also transport patients to medical facilities (Bureau of Labor Statistics). The BLS also explains that the job can be stressful and physically hard, and that workers may have to work nights, weekends, and holidays (Bureau of Labor Statistics). The CareerOneStop source also shows that people entering this field need to pay attention to training, licensing, or certification requirements (CareerOneStop). Together, these sources show that becoming an EMT is not just about wanting to help people. It also requires training, careful preparation, and an understanding of the working conditions.
